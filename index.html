Tribute to Stephen Hawking

Stephen Hawking

A Brilliant Theoretical Physicist

Hawking taking a zero-gravity flight in a "Vomit Comet"

Here's a timeline of Stephen Hawking:

  • 1942Born on January 8, 1942 in Oxford, England
  • 1958Attended St. Alban School until graduating in 1958, where he was a good student- but not great
  • Hawking then attended college at Oxford. His passion was math, his degree was in natural sciences
  • At Oxford, Hawking coxed a rowing team. Why? His words: To relieve immense boredom
  • 1962 Hawking graduated from Oxford and went to Cambridge for his PhD. When he started, symptoms of Lou Gehrig's disease began
  • Hawking was diagnosed with the deteriorating disease and given two to three years to live
  • 1965 Using a cane to get down the aisle, Hawking married his wife, Jane
  • He received his PhD athe age of 23
  • And he embarked upon lifelong research and teaching at Cambridge
  • 1968 At age 26, Hawking was in a wheelchair full-time
  • And he had two children with his wife by the time he was 27
  • 1970 His illness worsened, his research intensified
  • 1974 He proved that black holes aren't vacuums, coining the term Hawking Radiation.
  • He used his remaining speech capabilities to accept the first of what would be many honors
  • 1975 The Pius XI Gold Medal for Science for black hole discoveries
  • 1976 The Hughes Medal for outstanding original ideas
  • 1978 Albert Einstein Award, for extended studies on Einstein's work
  • 1985 Lost ability to speak. Began using a machine to talk for him, at first with a working finger, and later with a muscle in his cheek
  • 1988 Published A Brief History of Time. Since then; 25 million copies sold, translated in 40 different languages.
  • Winner of the Wolf Prize for assisting mankind
  • 1989 Prince of Asturias Award for notable discoveries
  • 1994 Discusses his personal life alongside lectures on thermodynamics of black holes and more in 1994's Black Holes and Baby Universes other Essays
  • 2006 Copley Medal of the Royal Society for outstanding scientific research
  • Divorced 2nd wife and grew close to his family in 2006. Co-published a science book for children with his daugter, Lucy
  • 2007 At age 65: Hawking experienced space simulation at Kennedy Space Center
  • 2009 Hawking given highes civilian honor in the U.S.: Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2010 Co-authored The Grand Design
  • Before Hawking, the oldest ALS patients made it to 32 and 39 years of age
  • He's considered one of the most influential minds of out time. And he was supposed to die 40 years ago
My goal is simple. It is a complete understanding of the universe, why it is as it is and why it exists at all. — Stephen Hawking
